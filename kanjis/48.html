夷
  • Frequency:2324
  • Kun: えびす, えみし, ころ.す, たい.らげる
  • On: イ
  • Meaning: barbarian, savage, Ainu
  • Examples:
    WordReadingMeaning
    夷えびすpeoples formerly of northern Japan with distinct language and culture (i.e. the Ainu); provincial (i.e. a person who lives far from the city); brutish, unsophisticated warrior (esp. used by Kyoto samurai to refer to samurai from eastern Japan); foreigner, barbarian
    夷えびす, ゑびす, えべす, ひるこEbisu (god of fishing and commerce)
    蝦夷えぞ, えみしpeoples formerly of northern Japan with distinct language and culture (i.e. the Ainu); Yezo (northern part of Meiji-era Japan, esp. Hokkaido, but also Sakhalin and the Kuril Islands)
    攘夷じょういexpulsion of the foreigners

    夷人いじんbarbarian, devil
    夷俗いぞくcustoms of the barbarians
    夷狄いてきbarbarians, aliens
